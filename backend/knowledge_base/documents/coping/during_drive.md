# Coping Strategies for During the Drive

## Overview
When anxiety rises during a drive, having strategies ready helps you stay calm and safe.

## Level 1: Mild Anxiety (Stress Score < 0.3)

### Prevention Mode
- Keep your favorite calming music playing
- Maintain comfortable temperature
- Stay hydrated
- Use cruise control on highways if available

### Quick Resets
- Roll shoulders back
- Unclench your jaw
- Soften your grip on the wheel
- Take one deep breath

## Level 2: Moderate Anxiety (Stress Score 0.3-0.6)

### Active Calming
- Start box breathing (4-4-4-4)
- Use the steering wheel grounding technique
- Say an affirmation: "I am safe and in control"
- Focus on just the next mile/minute

### Mental Strategies
- Name 5 things you can see (5-4-3-2-1)
- Describe your surroundings out loud
- Listen actively to your audio
- Remind yourself: "This feeling is temporary"

## Level 3: High Anxiety (Stress Score > 0.6)

### Immediate Actions
- Signal and move to the slow lane if possible
- Use 4-7-8 breathing
- Talk to Serene - say what you're feeling
- Consider the next safe exit or place to pull over

### Safety First
- Reduce speed slightly (stay safe, not dangerously slow)
- Increase following distance
- Focus only on the car directly ahead
- If needed, pull over safely

## Level 4: Critical (Stress Score > 0.8)

### Pull Over Protocol
1. Signal and move right
2. Find a safe spot (parking lot, rest area, shoulder if necessary)
3. Turn on hazard lights
4. Put car in park
5. Take 10 slow breaths
6. Wait until you feel calmer before continuing

## Remember
- Pulling over is always an option
- Your safety is the priority
- Anxiety passes - it always does
- You have handled difficult moments before
